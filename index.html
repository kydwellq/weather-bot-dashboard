• Unfilled orders (thin liquidity) cost nothing — you only risk what actually fills

All Trade Gates (10 checks before any trade)
Every signal must pass ALL of these before real money is risked
1
Minimum Score
Signal score must be ≥ 0.35 (combines edge, probability, time, liquidity, diversity). Below this = noise, not signal.
2
Max Entry Price
Entry price must be ≤ $0.55. Above that, risk/reward is skewed — you're risking $0.55+ to win $0.45. Not worth it.
3
No Duplicate Markets
Can't bet on the same ticker twice. If you already hold NYC high above 54°F, another bet on the same market is blocked.
4
One Bet Per Event
Can't hold two bets from the same event (e.g., gas above $4.11 AND above $4.22). Only one can win — guaranteed loss on the other.
5
Max Open Trades
Fleet-wide cap of 8 open trades. Prevents over-diversification where you can't monitor what you own.
6
Minimum Expected Profit
Must expect ≥ 0.25% of bankroll ($0.25). If fees eat the edge, the trade isn't worth the risk.
7
Max Same Bot Per Cycle
Max 3 trades from the same bot per 30-min scan. Prevents one bot from dominating every cycle.
8
Per-Bot Trade Cap
Each bot has its own max open trades (Weather: 4, Finance: 3, Energy: 3, Economics: 2). Prevents all capital concentrating in one strategy.
9
Correlation Group Limit
Correlated bots (Weather + Energy share "physical" group) can't exceed 50% of bankroll combined. If heat drives both, one bad forecast loses on both.
10
Total Exposure Limit
All open trades combined can't exceed 20% of bankroll ($20.00). The rest stays in cash as a safety buffer.

Kelly tells you the mathematically optimal bet size. We use quarter-Kelly for safety — slower growth but much less risk of ruin.
Kelly Cap 15% of bankroll Kelly Multiplier 25% (quarter-Kelly) Max Exposure 50% of bankroll Max Drawdown 25% from peak
Formula: kelly_f = (p_win × payout - p_lose) / payout, then × 0.25, capped at 15%
Example: If win prob = 75%, payout = 2:1 → Kelly says bet 37.5% → quarter-Kelly = 9.4% → capped at 9.4% of bankroll
Drawdown halt: If balance drops 25% from its peak, all trading stops automatically until you manually review.

🚫 Bracket Dead Zone Rules
We avoid betting on brackets where the forecast lands right on the boundary — too risky, like flipping a coin.
⛔ If forecast is within 1σ of a bracket boundary → skip (probability too uncertain)
⚠️ If forecast is within 2σ → edge must be extra large to compensate for boundary risk
✅ If forecast is >2σ from boundaries → standard rules apply, trade normally
Why? If a bracket is "High ≥ 70°F" and our forecast is 70.5°F with σ = 2°F, a slight model error flips the outcome.
Dead zones protect us from the trades where being right or wrong is basically random.
🔄 Trade Execution Rules
