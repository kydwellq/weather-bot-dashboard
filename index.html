How Trades Work
The full decision pipeline — what the bot does every 60 seconds
What markets can it trade?
KXBTCD — Kalshi's Bitcoin Hourly Directional markets.
Each hour (24/7), Kalshi opens a set of YES/NO brackets like:
"Will BTC be above $84,000 at 2:00 PM ET?"
Strike prices are spaced $250 apart — so for one event hour, there might be 50-180 brackets at different price levels.
Settlement: CF Benchmarks BRTI — a 60-second average of BTC price at the exact hour mark.
Decision pipeline (every 60 seconds)
1. Get live BTC price — Coinbase REST API, polled every 5 seconds 2. Scan Kalshi KXBTCD markets — find all active hourly brackets within 4 hours of settlement 3. Pre-flight checks — before any math, skip if:
Observation window — first 15 min of each hour: watch only, don't trade
Macro blackout — within ±2h of FOMC/CPI/NFP release (hardcoded 2026 dates)
Weekly drawdown — week P&L hit -$10 → paused until Monday
4. Gather volatility data from 3 sources:
Deribit options IV (50% weight) — what the options market thinks vol is
Realized vol from price ticks (30%) — actual recent BTC movement
ATR from candles (20%) — average true range, measures price swings
5. Calculate fair probability — log-normal model asks:
"Given BTC is at $X, with Y% vol and Z momentum, what's the chance it's above $Strike in N hours?"
Uses Deribit vol smile (skew-aware) when available. BRTI sigma widening adds extra uncertainty for brackets within $200 of spot (Coinbase ≠ BRTI settlement). 6. Compare to Kalshi price — if our model says 72% but Kalshi asks 58¢, that's a 14% edge 7. Proximity + micro-momentum boost — near-the-money brackets (within $500 of BTC) get up to +3% edge boost. Our 5s Coinbase feed detects moves before Kalshi reprices.
If BTC moved ≥0.3% in 2 min (micro-momentum), boost amplified 1.5x — Kalshi book is likely stale.
8. Pick side — YES or NO, whichever has better EV per dollar. Min conviction: 55% 9. Size with Kelly — quarter-Kelly formula, capped at $1/trade 10. Run 6-gate validator — kill switch, drawdown, allocation cap, duplicate, spread, resolved checks 11. Place order — limit order on Kalshi, then record full logic chain in journal for learning
Window lifecycle (current-hour events)
T+0 to T+15 min — Observation: watch price action, don't enter
T+15 to T+30 min — Decision: primary entry window (10% min edge)
T+30 to T+55 min — Late entry: requires 2x edge (20% min)
T+55 to T+60 min — No entry: too close to settlement
When does it skip? (most cycles)
No markets within 4 hours · Observation window (first 15 min) · Macro blackout (±2h of FOMC/CPI/NFP) · Edge below 10% (20% for late entries) · Spread too wide (>5¢) · ATR too low (<$150) or too high (>$3,000) · Low volume (<$100/event) · Already holding a position · Daily budget spent · Daily loss limit (-$3) · Weekly drawdown (-$10) · Cooldown after 2 consecutive losses (2h pause) · Kill switch on · Weekend low-liquidity hours (Sat/Sun 8PM-4AM ET)
Edge theory: latency + proximity
The bot's real edge isn't just the log-normal model — Kalshi market makers use the same Deribit IV. The differentiator is speed: our 5-second Coinbase feed detects BTC moves before Kalshi's order book reprices, especially for near-the-money brackets.

Proximity scoring boosts edge for brackets within $500 of current BTC price (where latency matters most). Micro-momentum (≥0.3% move in 2 min) amplifies the boost 1.5x because it signals the Kalshi book is stale. Meanwhile, BRTI sigma widening adds model uncertainty for ATM brackets (Coinbase ≠ BRTI), preventing false confidence on close calls.

📊 Edge & Conviction Thresholds
A trade must pass ALL of these to be considered. If any one fails, the trade is skipped.
Min Edge (global) 20% Min Conviction 60% Max Forecast Horizon 168h (7 days) Min Volume $0 (waived for weather)
Edge = how much our probability differs from Kalshi's price. 20% means we think a bet is worth ≥20¢ more than the market says.
Conviction = our estimated probability of winning. 60% means we only bet when we think there's a 60%+ chance we're right.
Backtest insight: All losses came from coin-flip bets (42% conviction). Wins clustered at 79%+ conviction.
💰 Position Sizing (Kelly Criterion)
Kelly tells you the mathematically optimal bet size. We use quarter-Kelly for safety — slower growth but much less risk of ruin.
Kelly Cap 8% of bankroll Kelly Multiplier 12.5% (eighth-Kelly) Max Exposure 50% of bankroll Max Drawdown 25% from peak
Formula: kelly_f = (p_win × payout - p_lose) / payout, then × 0.25, capped at 15%
Example: If win prob = 75%, payout = 2:1 → Kelly says bet 37.5% → quarter-Kelly = 9.4% → capped at 9.4% of bankroll
Drawdown halt: If balance drops 25% from its peak, all trading stops automatically until you manually review.

🚫 Bracket Dead Zone Rules
We avoid betting on brackets where the forecast lands right on the boundary — too risky, like flipping a coin.
⛔ If forecast is within 1σ of a bracket boundary → skip (probability too uncertain)
⚠️ If forecast is within 2σ → edge must be extra large to compensate for boundary risk
✅ If forecast is >2σ from boundaries → standard rules apply, trade normally
Why? If a bracket is "High ≥ 70°F" and our forecast is 70.5°F with σ = 2°F, a slight model error flips the outcome.
Dead zones protect us from the trades where being right or wrong is basically random.
⚖️ Hedge Trade Analysis
When a new signal conflicts directionally with an existing bet in the same event, the bot runs a risk analysis instead of an automatic block.
⚖️ Hedge min edge: 40% (2× the normal threshold — conflicting bets need much stronger conviction)
⚖️ Hedge min probability: 55% (must be confident the new signal will win)
⚖️ Kelly penalty: 60% reduction (bet size cut by 60% vs normal)
✅ Both-win zone: When brackets overlap (e.g., NO on B51.5 + YES on B53.5), there's a temperature range where both bets profit simultaneously
Why allow hedges? Weather conditions change. If models now strongly disagree with our original bet direction (64% edge!), the new data likely has information our original bet didn't have. Blocking a high-conviction signal just because we have an existing bet means leaving money on the table.
Protection: The 40% edge + 55% probability minimums ensure we only hedge when the new signal is extremely strong — not on marginal calls.
🔄 Trade Execution Rules
📝 Order type: Limit orders only — never market orders. We set our price, wait for fill.
⏱️ Fill timeout: 30 seconds — if nobody takes our limit order, it's cancelled automatically.
📊 Partial fills: If only some contracts fill, we keep what filled and cancel the rest.
💲 Side selection: Always pick the side (YES/NO) with the higher expected payout — never biased toward YES or NO.
🎯 Entry cap: Never pay more than 85¢ per contract — high prices leave no room for profit after fees.
🔁 Scan interval: Every 15 minutes — checks for new markets, settles completed trades, re-evaluates positions.
